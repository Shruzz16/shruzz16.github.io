Modeled and analyzed a **planetary gearbox assembly** in **SolidWorks** to study compact torque transmission through a
**sun gear, planet gears, ring gear, and carrier** arrangement. The project focused on connecting **CAD assembly design**
with **gear-train kinematics, manufacturability, fit, and tolerance control**.

---

## Project overview
- Built a complete planetary gearbox assembly with:
  - **sun gear**
  - **planet gears**
  - **internal ring gear**
  - **carrier**
  - **shaft / pin interfaces**
- Verified proper **gear meshing**, **alignment**, and **assembly feasibility**
- Organized the design for compact packaging and smooth rotational motion

---

## Design objectives
- Create a compact gearbox layout for efficient torque transmission
- Ensure proper mating and meshing between all gears
- Maintain manufacturable geometry and assembly-friendly part interfaces
- Evaluate the relationship between **gear geometry** and **mechanical performance**

---

## CAD and assembly development
- Modeled the gearbox components in **SolidWorks**
- Assembled the full transmission system using rotational and concentric mates
- Checked:
  - **gear interference**
  - **clearance**
  - **alignment**
  - **carrier fit**
  - **shaft and pin placement**
- Structured the assembly to reflect real mechanical packaging constraints

---

## Engineering calculations
Using the gearbox geometry, performed key design calculations including:
- **gear ratio**
- **pitch diameters**
- **center distance**
- **speed reduction**
- **torque multiplication**
- **load sharing among planet gears**

For the selected configuration:
- **Sun gear teeth** = 12
- **Planet gear teeth** = 24
- **Ring gear teeth** = 60
- **Module** = 1
- **Face width** = 10 mm
- **Number of planets** = 3

Basic planetary condition verified:
- **Zr = Zs + 2Zp**
- **60 = 12 + 2(24)**

For the common case of **sun input, ring fixed, carrier output**:
- **Reduction ratio = 6:1**

This was used to connect geometry with expected output speed and torque behavior.
